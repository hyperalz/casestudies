The platform combines automation, AI, and data science across online grocery operations: e-commerce, supply chain, fulfilment, and last-mile delivery. Background

Ocado Technology has been building online grocery solutions for over 25 years. The Ocado Smart Platform (OSP) is their end-to-end solution that uses automation, AI, and data science across the entire online grocery operation, from e-commerce and supply chain to fulfilment and delivery.

Retailers can pick and choose which OSP components they need. The platform does over 70 million supply chain calculations daily to keep availability high and accuracy tight. Right now it's running operations for 13 partners across 25 live sites and 1,000+ stores, hitting 99.4% order accuracy with NPS scores of 7580% for partners in 2024.

When I joined in 2013, Ocado Technology was at a turning point. They needed to move from project-based work to a product-led approach, and they were growing fast with international expansion on the horizon. I was their first Product hire, coming in as Product Owner and Product Operations lead reporting to the General Manager. The company had 600+ software developers and 22 Product Owners running three legacy systems, all while trying to replatform everything into what became OSP, a modular platform for both online-only and omnichannel grocery retail.

Challenge

Ocado needed to shift from internal project management to a product-centric culture to support global expansion. The main challenges were:

  • Maintaining legacy systems While building a new platform that would eventually encompass 59 interconnected applications.
  • Aligning a large, distributed team Around agile practices, with resistance to change from traditional methods.
  • Mitigating a 50m risk To their first international client by ensuring the nascent OSP was battle-tested in real-world scenarios, without passing on unproven elements.
  • Fostering collaboration Across departments, coaching inexperienced Product Owners, and establishing an operational roadmap amid ambiguity and high-stakes delivery pressures.
  • Balancing innovation with empirical feedback To drive continuous improvement, all while scaling to support hyper-efficient operations. This included automated Customer Fulfilment Centres (CFCs) that could simulate 270 years of operations in just 12 months, requiring sophisticated modelling and validation approaches.
  • Ensuring platform reliability For mission-critical operations where system failures could impact millions of customer orders, requiring rigorous testing, monitoring, and failover capabilities across the entire platform stack.

Solution

I led the cultural and operational shift by:

Coaching & Mentoring

I pushed the move from project to product management, coaching 20+ Product Owners through communities of practice, workshops, and one-on-ones. Also mentored Heads of Department and IT Team Leads on agile practices and product thinking.

Strategic Direction & Lifecycle Management

I set the strategic direction for the platform and managed the replatforming while keeping legacy systems running. That meant handling planning, scope, budget, risks, and issues across multiple workstreams that all depended on each other.

Internal Startup & Dogfooding

I set up an internal "startup" within Ocado Technology, basically a fake retailer so we could "eat our own dog food." This let us test OSP in real-world conditions before it was ready, getting actual feedback without risking our first international client. That safe space was crucial for managing the 50m risk.

Agile Practices & Ways of Working

I rolled out Scrum and Kanban across product and project delivery, focusing on transparency and continuous discovery. Set up communities of practice and regular workshops to get these practices embedded. Had to work through a lot of resistance from people used to traditional project management.

Operational Roadmap

I built and delivered an operational roadmap that was fed into product and team-level Kanban. I facilitated the delivery of the Ocado Smart Platform with a portfolio-level view, enabling me to see retailer needs manifest as solutions (facilitated with Product Owners) and roll out across individual teams and modules.

Results

Platform Launch

Successfully launched OSP, preventing the 50m risk transfer to the first client and enabling Ocado to become a platform business.

Team Transformation

I coached teams to use product-led practices, which sped up delivery and innovation. Ocado Retail became consistently profitable and grew faster than anyone else in the UK.

Platform Scale & Innovation

We scaled the platform to 59 interconnected applications, handling 10 billion daily product predictions. Ocado Technology now has 2,500+ technologists and 3,000+ patents, an IP portfolio 10 times bigger than the entire UK grocery industry.

Business Value

Delivered measurable business value: High NPS scores (7580% for partners in 2024), reduced substitutions, and a partnership model with dedicated expert support.

Awards & Recognition

Ocado Retail achieved awards like Online Service of the Year at The Grocer Gold Awards (July 2025).

Operational Excellence

We hit 98% on-time dispatch, kept waste down to 0.49%, and run 100 million optimisation calculations per second. The platform's AI supply chain tools keep availability high and costs low.

Key Takeaways

That experience demonstrated the practical value of agile and product thinking in large, high-stakes settings. By creating space for safe experimentation, using an internal startup approach, and prioritising real user feedback, the team mitigated a 50 million client risk while accelerating delivery.

It also shaped my understanding of leading without formal authority. I built alignment through coaching, communities of practice, and cross-functional collaboration. Cultural and mindset shifts proved as important as technical ones.

Today, OSP supports global retailers with efficient platform operations. Its continued success illustrates how early investment in product thinking, agile practices, and real-world validation can deliver lasting results in platform businesses.

A day that shifted the conversation for Caerphilly Council's Housing Team

On 21 October 2024, Alex Osborne stepped into a meeting room at Caerphilly County Borough Council to run a full-day workshop for the Housing Team. As director of product-led transformation at Foam Works Digital, he had worked with the council on several digital projects. This session focused on the rollout of Civica Cx, the new housing management system meant to streamline repairs, tenant support, and case handling.

Background

The team dealt daily with homelessness prevention, property maintenance, and rising demand in one of Wales's larger boroughs. They needed a space to speak openly about what worked and what did not.

Challenge

The Civica implementation faced real hurdles. A scheduler module, essential for repairs, might not arrive by March 2025. Relations with the supplier had cooled. Modules remained incomplete, data lacked accuracy, and the project weighed on staff morale. Training lagged, and deadlines loomed with risks of non-compliance.

The team's vision statement "To deliver an outstanding high quality service for all Caerphilly customers" felt safe but uninspiring. Metrics stayed inward-looking, missing real-time insight or stories of lives changed. Assumptions about time savings and automation went untested.

Solution

Alex built the day around a 20-point agenda, starting with ground rules taped to the wall: no hierarchy in the room, equal value for every voice, openness, and "you get out what you put in."

Participants filled flipcharts with sticky notes. They mapped why the team existed serving customers, providing homes, meeting Welsh Government goals. They listed problems: poverty, vulnerability, poor housing stock, overwhelming demand.

Discussions turned to success measures. Alex pushed for fewer, sharper metrics tied to human outcomes, like "five fewer people homeless in Caerphilly today," alongside qualitative case studies.

He challenged the vision gently, suggesting a bolder North Star: ending homelessness in the borough, even if time-bound. The room responded with ideas like "every vulnerable person safe and secure."

Talk shifted to Civica's specific fixes single customer view, automation and expected gains, quantified where possible. Hypothesis emerged: the system could lift annual case processing from 2,000 to 3,000.

Pain points surfaced: supplier friction, missing features, staff well-being. Immediate priorities included securing the scheduler, wider team involvement in testing, and training.

Solutions followed: map current and future architecture, learn from Monmouthshire Council's experience, form a working group to confront Civica with evidence, seek quotes from alternatives like Total Connect or ArcGIS.

Results

By day's end, the team had a refreshed sense of purpose. They carried forward concrete steps: architectural maps, cross-council benchmarking, a focused working group, and procurement options. Risks like morale and compliance gained clear mitigation paths.

The workshop rebuilt momentum around Civica while tying technical changes to broader impact on residents.

Key Takeaways

A single structured day with honest conversation can realign a public sector team. Linking daily tools to ambitious outcomes reducing homelessness, securing homes turns implementation struggles into shared drive. Data-backed challenges to suppliers and bold vision-setting prove essential in constrained environments.

Shoogle aimed to create a light-touch mobile app and platform for community-driven motivation, combining needs/goals, communities, reflection, habits, rewards, one-second pulses, and real-time dashboardsemphasising connection over mere measurement in a $34.2B global Health/Welltech market (projected to $67.7B by 2023).

Challenge

Building Shoogle presented multiple significant challenges:

  • High development costs from major vendors (e.g., 1m quotes) Made traditional approaches unaffordable, requiring innovative, cost-effective solutions.
  • Limited resources and multi-role coverage In a small team, requiring comprehensive ownership across research, strategy, planning, scope, budget, and risk management.
  • Need for lean, remote near-shore management To deliver the platform cost-effectively while maintaining quality and alignment.
  • Building real-time pulse data, community features, and automated insights Required sophisticated technical capabilities including data processing, feature extraction, and model training.
  • Market fragmentation, regulatory hurdles, and slow adoption In Health/Welltech, requiring careful navigation of compliance and market dynamics.
  • Product/market fit amid competition In chat, surveys, and rewards spaces, requiring clear differentiation and value proposition.

Solution

We leveraged four months of qualitative user research to build an MVP with empowering features. The approach included:

User Research

Conducted four months of qualitative user research to understand hidden needs, WFH challenges, and what truly motivates employees, informing the MVP design and features.

MVP Features

Built unique/unlimited communities, private buddies/journaling, reminders, peer rewards, and non-intrusive swipes for pulse data across wellbeing dimensions (social, physical, mental, etc.).

End-to-End Leadership

Provided full programme leadership for iOS/Android delivery, covering all roles except development for comprehensive ownershipresearch, strategy, planning, scope, budget, and risk.

LLM Integration

Exploited Large Language Models for data insights and automation, including data cleaning/pre-processing, feature extraction, model training (e.g., Naive Bayes, GPT-3 for sentiment), and real-time dashboards.

Lean Near-Shore Team

Managed a remote near-shore development team of 5, delivering the full platform from consultancy/R&D to launch-ready MVP in a focused timeframe.

Market Engagement

Pitched to HR, benefits managers, and healthcare providers via podcasts, trade shows, and demos, building traction and early adopter interest.

Key Takeaways

The Shoogle project showed that a capable wellbeing platform can reach market for far less than traditional vendor estimates. Built for 35,000 against quotes of 1 million, the app emerged from four months of lean agile development, supported by a small remote near-shore team and full-cycle product leadership.

Extensive qualitative research uncovered what employees often keep hidden and what actually drives their motivation. Features shifted from simple tracking to fostering genuine connection.

Large language models handled data analysis and automation, enabling the system to interpret pulse responses, spot emerging issues, and suggest targeted interventions.

Product leadership spanned research, strategy, planning, scoping, budgeting, and risk management, maintaining control within tight constraints.

Regular updates and transparent collaboration kept the compact team aligned and allowed rapid adjustments.

Leadership that welcomed openness and personal disclosure mirrored the app's own purpose, strengthening team motivation and shifting focus toward prevention rather than reaction.

The result positions Shoogle to enter a $34.2 billion market with solid projected finances and clear commercial viability.
