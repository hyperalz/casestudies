Transforming Ocado Technology from Project to Product Management

I was Ocado Technology's first Product hire. My job was to shift the organisation from a project-based model to a product-led approach, which enabled the launch of the Ocado Smart Platform (OSP). The platform combines automation, AI, and data science across online grocery operations: e-commerce, supply chain, fulfilment, and last-mile delivery. Today it powers 13 global partners across 25+ sites.

Background

Ocado Technology has been building online grocery solutions for over 25 years. The Ocado Smart Platform (OSP) is their end-to-end solution that uses automation, AI, and data science across the entire online grocery operation, from e-commerce and supply chain to fulfilment and delivery.

Retailers can pick and choose which OSP components they need. The platform does over 70 million supply chain calculations daily to keep availability high and accuracy tight. Right now it's running operations for 13 partners across 25 live sites and 1,000+ stores, hitting 99.4% order accuracy with NPS scores of 75–80% for partners in 2024.

When I joined in 2013, Ocado Technology was at a turning point. They needed to move from project-based work to a product-led approach, and they were growing fast with international expansion on the horizon. I was their first Product hire, coming in as Product Owner and Product Operations lead reporting to the General Manager. The company had 600+ software developers and 22 Product Owners running three legacy systems, all while trying to replatform everything into what became OSP, a modular platform for both online-only and omnichannel grocery retail.

Challenge

Ocado needed to shift from internal project management to a product-centric culture to support global expansion. The main challenges were:

  • Maintaining legacy systems while building a new platform that would eventually encompass 59 interconnected applications.
  • Aligning a large, distributed team around agile practices, with resistance to change from traditional methods.
  • Mitigating a £50m risk to their first international client by ensuring the nascent OSP was battle-tested in real-world scenarios, without passing on unproven elements.
  • Fostering collaboration across departments, coaching inexperienced Product Owners, and establishing an operational roadmap amid ambiguity and high-stakes delivery pressures.
  • Balancing innovation with empirical feedback to drive continuous improvement, all while scaling to support hyper-efficient operations. This included automated Customer Fulfilment Centres (CFCs) that could simulate 270 years of operations in just 12 months, requiring sophisticated modelling and validation approaches.
  • Ensuring platform reliability for mission-critical operations where system failures could impact millions of customer orders, requiring rigorous testing, monitoring, and failover capabilities across the entire platform stack.

Solution

I led the cultural and operational shift by:

Coaching & Mentoring

I pushed the move from project to product management, coaching 20+ Product Owners through communities of practice, workshops, and one-on-ones. Also mentored Heads of Department and IT Team Leads on agile practices and product thinking.

Strategic Direction & Lifecycle Management

I set the strategic direction for the platform and managed the replatforming while keeping legacy systems running. That meant handling planning, scope, budget, risks, and issues across multiple workstreams that all depended on each other.

Internal Startup & Dogfooding

I set up an internal "startup" within Ocado Technology, basically a fake retailer so we could "eat our own dog food." This let us test OSP in real-world conditions before it was ready, getting actual feedback without risking our first international client. That safe space was crucial for managing the £50m risk.

Agile Practices & Ways of Working

I rolled out Scrum and Kanban across product and project delivery, focusing on transparency and continuous discovery. Set up communities of practice and regular workshops to get these practices embedded. Had to work through a lot of resistance from people used to traditional project management.

Operational Roadmap

I built and delivered an operational roadmap that matched business goals, using Impact Mapping, BDD, and OKRs to figure out what to prioritise.

Results

Platform Launch

Successfully launched OSP, preventing the £50m risk transfer to the first client and enabling Ocado to become a platform business.

Team Transformation

I coached teams to use product-led practices, which sped up delivery and innovation. Ocado Retail became consistently profitable and grew faster than anyone else in the UK.

Platform Scale & Innovation

We scaled the platform to 59 interconnected applications, handling 10 billion daily product predictions. Ocado Technology now has 2,500+ technologists and 3,000+ patents, an IP portfolio 10 times bigger than the entire UK grocery industry.

Business Value

Delivered measurable business value: High NPS scores (75–80% for partners in 2024), reduced substitutions, and a partnership model with dedicated expert support.

Awards & Recognition

Ocado Retail achieved awards like Online Service of the Year at The Grocer Gold Awards (July 2025).

Operational Excellence

We hit 98% on-time dispatch, kept waste down to 0.49%, and run 100 million optimisation calculations per second. The platform's AI supply chain tools keep availability high and costs low.

Key Takeaways

This work showed me how powerful agile and product thinking can be in large-scale, mission-critical environments. Creating that safe experimentation space (the internal startup) and focusing on real feedback helped us manage the £50m client risk and move faster. It also reinforced my approach to leading without authority: building teams through coaching, communities of practice, and collaboration. The cultural and mindset shifts mattered just as much as the technical changes. OSP's success today, powering global retailers with market-leading efficiency, shows that investing early in product thinking, agile practices, and real-world validation pays off in platform businesses.

I was part of the senior leadership team leading product transformation to build a modern FX and Hedging Analytics Platform from scratch. This was a greenfield project in a complex, regulated banking environment. We modernised legacy operations that ran on manual processes and spreadsheets, building currency management, dynamic hedging, and risk analytics for corporate clients. The work led to Best Business FX Provider awards in 2024 and 2025.

Background

Investec Bank is a specialist bank offering Treasury and Risk Management solutions, including Corporate FX services to help businesses manage currency volatility, hedging, and international payments. Their digital platform, Investec ix, includes FX analytics for 24-hour position oversight, dynamic hedging to automate programs, online trading, and global payment execution.

Investec won Best Business FX Provider in the MoneyFacts Awards (2024 and 2025) and Best FX Trading Platform in 2025 from Global Finance Magazine. They combine high-touch dealer support with analytics to deliver clear insights, risk modelling, and operational efficiency for businesses dealing with financial uncertainty.

I joined Investec in 2017 as a Consultant in Product-Led Transformation and Delivery for Corporate & Investment Banking. We built a new team with its own P&L to create a modern FX and Hedging Analytics Platform from scratch. The goal was to replace legacy operations that ran on manual processes, spreadsheets, and siloed systems. This had to happen in a complex, regulated banking environment where innovation needs to work alongside strict compliance.

Challenge

Investec needed to modernise FX and hedging operations as demand grew for faster, more efficient risk management. The main challenges were:

  • Replatforming an outdated estate that ran on manual processes, spreadsheets, and siloed systems. This slowed everything down and increased operational risk.
  • Growing the team from 7 to 30 while getting agile processes into a traditional banking culture that didn't want to change.
  • Building a client-facing platform for corporate FX, hedging analytics, and payments, with tools for dynamic hedging, risk modelling, and international transactions.
  • Moving fast in a regulated environment. We built a Business Savings account in 12 weeks with 12 people. Goldman Sachs took 2 years with 200+ developers. This meant shifting from "prototyping addiction" to actually shipping production code.
  • Working through ambiguity in a high-stakes sector, balancing regulatory compliance with innovation, and getting traditionally siloed teams to collaborate on continuous delivery. This took persistent "messy" change work: breaking old patterns, challenging assumptions, and building momentum through diagrams, governance structures, and multi-stream channels.

Solution

As part of the senior leadership team, I led product and delivery. Here's what we did:

Agile Processes

I rolled out agile product development processes, built cross-functional teams, and coached on continuous discovery and empiricism using Scrum, Kanban, and BDD.

Team Growth & Development

I led hiring and grew the team from 7 to 30, mentoring on agile practices and product thinking. Pushed an Open API strategy to improve collaboration and break down silos, building a culture of continuous learning in a traditionally hierarchical banking environment.

Rapid Replatforming

I led the complete replatforming of the entire estate in 4 months, a tight timeline for a regulated environment. We moved low-value trades online, turned complex manual structuring work from spreadsheets into software, and kept client operations running throughout.

Client-Collaborative Features

I delivered features like the Hedging Analytics tool by working closely with clients. This enabled dynamic hedging to cut operational work and provide 24-hour FX position oversight. The client-centric approach meant we built what they actually needed while staying compliant.

Continuous Delivery

I implemented Continuous Delivery to speed up releases, while managing end-to-end lifecycle including planning, risks, and stakeholder alignment.

Results

Platform Launch

We replatformed the estate in 4 months and launched the client-facing FX and Hedging Analytics Platform, modernising legacy operations and enabling seamless currency management.

Innovative Platform Features

We delivered tools including dynamic hedging, FX analytics, online trading, and global payments. These improved risk visibility, enabled proactive risk identification, and made operations more efficient for corporate clients managing currency exposure.

Rapid Delivery

We built a Business Savings account in 12 weeks, showing we could move fast and beat industry benchmarks.

High-Performing Team

We built a high-performing team that adopted agile practices, leading to faster releases, better collaboration, and recognition for Investec's award-winning FX services.

Business Outcomes

We improved business outcomes through proactive risk identification, clear insights that non-financial professionals can understand, and integrated services that combine FX expertise with broader banking capabilities. The platform lets businesses control their hedging strategies while getting expert support.

Award Recognition

Best Business FX Provider in the MoneyFacts Awards (2024 and 2025), showing the platform's success and market leadership.

Key Takeaways

This work showed me the value of blending high-touch leadership with digital innovation in regulated environments. By focusing on cultural transformation through persistent coaching, cross-functional teams, and empirical processes, we turned ambiguity into competitive advantage and delivered value faster than industry benchmarks. The "messy" change work was critical: breaking old patterns, challenging traditional banking culture, and building collaboration despite stretch goals. This built resilient, high-performing teams that work well in complex situations. The Best Business FX Provider (2024-2025) and Best FX Trading Platform (2025) awards show that cultural shifts, combined with technical excellence, create real competitive advantages in regulated sectors.
